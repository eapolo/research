About

I completed my B.Sc. in Agricultural Engineering in 2016 at the University of Seville (US), where I received an award for distinguished academic performance. During my B.Sc. program, I had the opportunity to spend three months at The Andalusian Institute of Agricultural and Fisheries Research and Training (IFAPA), where I published my first peer-reviewed paper and developed a passion for research. In 2017, I earned two master's degrees, each accompanied by an extraordinary end-of-studies award. Throughout my master's training, I received three grants from the Fundación de Investigación de la US for research collaboration under Dr. José María Urbano Fuentes-Guerra and Dr. Manuel Pérez-Ruíz. In 2018, I secured a research internship contract through the "Contratación de personal investigador en el marco del Programa de Garantía Juvenil en la US." That same year, I was awarded a PIF (Personal Investigador en Formación) Fellowship from the US to begin my Ph.D., which I completed in 2022. My doctoral research focused on integrating new technologies in agriculture, specifically on artificial intelligence (AI), drones, phenotyping, and remote sensing. I spent six months at Wageningen University & Research (Netherlands), collaborating with Dr. João Valente on drone-based projects for yield estimations in apple orchards as part of my predoctoral research. Additionally, I contributed to several research projects under the guidance of Dr. Manuel Pérez-Ruiz and Dr. Gregorio Egea, aiming to apply AI in precision farming, crop monitoring, LiDAR sensors, remote sensing, robotics, IoT, and satellite imagery. Before defending my thesis, I began a postdoctoral position at Ghent University's Faculty of Bioscience Engineering, investigating the use of hyperspectral imagery and AI for crop monitoring and disease detection under Dr. Abdul M. Mouazen's supervision. I then served as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Data Science Institute (Hasselt University) and as a Project Coordinator at the Flanders AI Academy, collaborating with Dr. Dirk Valkenborg. In January 2024, I started a postdoctoral researcher role at KU Leuven in the Division of Forest, Nature, and Landscape at the Department of Earth & Environmental Sciences, under the guidance of Dr. Stef Lhermite and Dr. Ben Somers. The primary focus is on using Generative Adversarial Neural Networks (GANs) and satellite images to predict changes in vegetation due to climate change impact. Thanks to the PIF Fellowship from the US, I gained significant teaching experience at both Bachelor's and Master's levels, totaling 264 hours of official lessons and 167 hours of lifelong learning teaching experience in various national and international courses. To date, I have published 12 papers in peer-reviewed journals, 4 articles in press & outreach journals, delivered more than 10 oral presentations at national and international conferences, and authored a book and a book chapter. Additionally, I have supervised and co-supervised several MSc (6) and B.Sc (5) theses and one Ph.D. thesis. From a research quality standpoint, I have an h-index of 8 with 318 citations according to Scopus and an h-index of 9 with 466 citations according to Google Scholar.
